Kira Yoshikage Introduction

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure Season 4

This is a very unique scene for a villain's introduction in a fighting / action anime. Often villains are decided to be malicious, evil, savage, etc. However, Kira Yoshikage is a very unique villain in comparison. This is a villain where he hates to fight. He is looking for a very quiet and normal life. He values an ordinary life like a random social person you can find anywhere on the street, with a little hobby of killing young women. A very contradictory mindset, but makes sense for this villain character. In the past 15 years, he has murdered hundreds without getting detected, because his stand ability can literally blow up the body into nothingness, not even atoms of ashes, literally nothingness, so there is no way for the legal system to judge or find his crime. This little introduction is so normal, too normal that it sounds kind of creepy. This is the first time that Kira has been discovered, therefore he is calmly introducing himself, and the second the introduction is over, he murders this kid without leaving any trace. Throughout this entire encounter, Kira showed no emotions of anger or frustrations, he is gently showing his ability to the audience while leaving a significant imagery. The music played here fits so well with this character. A very calm and ordinary person at glance, but the more you look into him, you would realize the darkness and sin he has made throughout all these years. To the majority of the citizens, nobody even knew such a threat existed. Only the main characters experienced this hard fought battle against a Hannibal with super powers.

Saito vs 70,000 Army

Zero No Tsukaima





This is a very old anime, and yet is one of the first ones I have ever watched back in elementary. Zero no Tsukaima is one of the anime that perfectly combines romance with fighting. Saito is a Tsukaima, which is a servant pet for Louis, the main female character of the series. This world has a very serious difference in hierarchy between common and noble people. Commons are the ones who can’t have magic, and nobles can. Saito is a very special Tsikaima, since he is a human instead of an animal, and he is common in this world. In the beginning, every noble around, including Louis didn’t like him, basically treated him as an animal. However, Saito really used his genuine emotions and feelings that changed everyone around him, making everyone think that commons can also be amazing. This clip specifically is Saito trying to buy time for the nobles and the kings to retreat from a war. Therefore, he knocked out Louis, and lied to everyone that he will be back in a few minutes, while sending them off. He stayed on the battlefield, single handedly facing off against a 70 thousand army, who had magic, arrows, cannons, with a single sword. In the end we know that he survived the tragedy, but the moment when he decided to sacrifice himself for his beloved one, really made me view him as a Man. Keep in mind, he is only 17 in this clip. This is not necessarily a popular anime, but I still think this is a good clip to watch.

Dio vs Jotaro: First Encounter

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure Season 3

This is a scene originating from JOJO’s Bizarre Adventure: Season three part 2, Stardust Crusaders Egypt saga. This is the very first direct encounter from the major character Kujo Jotaro and the main Villain Dio. Throughout the whole series, Jotaro has always been the mastermind of the team, where he uses his intelligence and strength to help the crew overcome many difficulties. However, Dio, as the main and final Boss of the series is no joke in strength and abilities. With many of his fellow comrades sacrificed against Dio, it is impossible for Jotaro to remain calm against Dio, even though he acknowledges the overwhelming power within this villain. As the audience, we are also excited to see how our main character goes against this powerful villain. The excitement built up to this point is incredible, and we can also see the great animations played in this clip. The shading and color skim really show the emotions from the characters. Different from the other characters trying their best to step away from Dio’s Stand, Jotaro simply said: “How can I beat the crap out of you without getting close?” That line is so robust and daring to say, but really hypes and tension and setting up for their epic final battle. Not to mention that I still think this battle is the most iconic one out of the entire JoJo series. In addition, the voice acting between the two characters is so nice. Ono Daisuke, the voice of Jotaro really made this character come to life, and I can’t imagine anyone else that can fit the role. However, Koyasu Takehito, voice of Dio, is probably the best male voice actor I know, to this date. In fact, his voice acting is iconic, Dio became his representation. The way he handled this character with the craziness and intelligence at the same time, really shows his capabilities as a voice actor.

Kirito Destroys Oberon

Sword Art Online Season 1





This is a scene from the first season of Sword Art Online, and it is the second last episode of the series. The entire first season is talking about Kirito, the main character trying to help himself and his friends escape this virtual reality game. In the first part of the series, after helping everyone escape this game known as sword art online, we thought everything was going to be alright, but not everyone escaped the game. A game administrator at the very last moment stole a bunch of SAO players as an experiment to invent artificial intelligence to make military weapons and sell them for money, this included Kirito’s girlfriend Asuna. Therefore, it took Kirito another 12 episodes to get to the place where Asuna was kidnapped. This fight against the game administrator, Sugou, otherwise known as Oberon, the kind of Elves, is the most disgusting, and most satisfying fight in the series. Sugou is having a marriage with Asuna in real life, and is trying to keep Asuna inside the game forever, since he is the game master of ALO. At the final battle Kirito witnesses all of the disguising moves Sugou is doing to Asuna right in front of him. After getting a message from the original game master, also known as the final boss of the last game, Kayaba. Kirito manages to steal the administrator level back to himself, and makes this satisfying scene between the king of thieves versus the gilded hero. The music builds up, and the animation showing Sugou losing his temper as he loses his power in the game really makes the scene really hype, and the moment we see Kirito lower the pain absorption level to 0, we know this is going to be a bloody revenge and demolishment.

Obito vs Kakashi

Naruto: Shippuden





This is not a very intense fight or some sort, however, the animations and camera work on this clip is probably the best in all anime history. This fight alone is a portrait of the lives and relationship between Obito and Kakshi. This is also a great description on the characteristics of both characters, while showing how they have grown as individuals, how the minds and goals have departed since the incident. They both share the same pair of eyes, and yet sees the world completely differently. They both are disciples from the same mentor, and are each other’s worst enemy. Kakshi saw the miracles from Naruto, how he is trusting his friends and everyone else. He understood and believed the future would be bright, and fought for it. Obito on the other hand only sees despair and darkness. He is only seeking to hide away from reality into a fake dream, while Kakashi is facing the challenges, for those he loved and his beliefs. The constant flashbacks show how something never changed, while some are much different. The music also makes this a fight that is unavoidable, both are simply approaching each other with all of their respect. They know each other like the back of their hands, and Kakashi knows that this is not only the fight to save the world, but to save his best friend. At the end, he said he had no choice but to kill Obito.

Saitama vs Boros Part 1

One Punch Man Season 1





This is a scene from One punch Man season one last episode, the final battle between Saitama and Boros. Saitama as the main character has the unbeatable strength of one punching everybody, and is facing off against another strong being in the universe Boros. They both share the same feeling of emptiness where no one is strong enough to challenge them, and Boros flew 20 years to Earth to seek for someone strong enough for him to try hard, and Saitama is the person. The animations are incredible in this clip and the fight itself is so dramatic and epic, I had to split it into two parts, and part one is covering the emotions and perspective from Boros. The music is a very strong representation for Boros, where he is hyped up, finding someone who is actually able to stand up to him in a fight, he is enjoying himself, you can see him smiling and having fun. He is able to test his limits, fighting with everything he has. This is the fight worth it enough for him to travel in a spaceship for 20 years. Although he lost in the end, he has fulfilled his life, and died in the best way he could ever imagine.

Saitama vs Boros Part 2

One Punch Man Season 1





Part two of the scene is a continuation of the fight, where this is coming from Saitama’s perspective. Although this seemed to be an epic and somewhat even battle, in the end, Saitama never tried. He never expected someone to stand up to him, and remained in a very calm attitude all the way through. He had hoped that this alien ruler could give him the excitement he wanted for so long, but turns out, once Saitama starts trying, everything falls apart. The sad music is a strong demonstration for Saitama. He won the battle, he beat possibly the strongest thing in the universe, and yet he didn’t feel the victory, he didn’t feel proud of himself at all. He can sense that this is probably the only person in the universe that shares the same emptiness with him, but still, he had to finish him off. In the last scene, Boros understood that Saitama must feel way more lonely than him, and was genuinely sad about that. It’s sad that Boros didn’t give him the challenge and Saitama is sad that he felt nothing different.

Satoshi & Greninjia vs Shota & Sceptile

Pokemon XY&Z





Pokémon has been one of my favorite anime series ever, which is mentioned in entry 2. Out of the entire series, my favorite would be Pokémon XYZ. This is one of the series where they spend a lot of time on making the fighting senses intense and hype, which to me seems to be the purpose of Pokémon. This fight in particular in the semi finals of the championship for this season. This rivalry between Shota and Satoshi has been one of the best ones amongst the entire series. In the past, Satoshi has always been the one who is trying to catch up to the other rival, for instance, in Diamond and Pearl, as well as the OG series. However, Shota, as a beginner has no experience with Pokémon, but he has been following Satoshi from his footprints, climbing all the way to his level which Satoshi admires during this battle. This series also made my favorite Pokémon aspect of all time, Mega Evolutions, making old Pokmeons shine once more in a new form in battle. Satoshi in particular has his own evolution with Greninjia. The bond between the two and cause and synchro, allowing both to gain an insane power up in battle. This battle also had my favorite Pokémon OST of all time, sung by the voice actor of Satoshi himself. The build to this battle, and how it ended up really can be seen as the final championship battle, but unfortunately, it is only the semi finals, and I don’t want to talk about the final battle. It’s probably the worst fumble ever done by the Pokémon company.

Satoshi & Pikachu vs Dande & Charizard

Pokemon Journey





This clip is probably the best clip in all Pokémon history. Although the Season itself is really bad, with very questionable animations and graphics, as a Pokémon fan, we can live with it. As someone who has watched Satoshi from his first day leaving the town, on to this 25 year long journey, it is so emotional seeing him holding an actual golden trophy on the stage. All these years, the company wants Satoshi to never end his journey by letting him lose every time, showing that they are more for him to grow, I get it. However, after all these years, we can see how Satoshi has become a better trainer, there are people even viewing him as someone to look up to, Shota, for example, and yet, he has never won an actual tournament. At last, the eight master’s league is the best opportunity to show that Satoshi can truly become the best trainer in the world. All the championships from different regions across the world come and join this fight, and Satoshi is beating every single one of them. The build up to this moment took way too long, 25 years, many of us have grown from kids to adults, including myself, and yet, Satoshi is still 10. All the Pokémons, all the friends he has met throughout all these years are cheering him up, wanting him to win, and at last, he didn’t fail us. He has finally become the Pokémon master, he has always wanted to be. The man standing at the top with his Pikachu.

Goku Going Super Saiyan

Dragon Ball Z





This is probably the most iconic scene in all anime history. Goku going Super Saiyan on Namek is the most intense and most outrageous scene ever. Frieza, as one the most iconic Dragon Ball villains ever, or even one of the most iconic villains in all anime, really demonstrates the feeling of frighten and despair to the audience. Such a strong opponent caused a lot of sacrifices in the last past 30 years, and our main character Goku, was always the hope in the crew, who always stood up against any challenge, tried his best making the spirit Bomb, but still couldn’t elimite Frieza. When Frieza showed up on the rock, I was shocked and genuinely felt terrified in front of the screen. When he shot Piccolo through the chest, I thought: Oh no, we came all this way for nothing. His elimination of Krillin, Goku’s best friend, triggered Goku to go through this raging transformation. From the voice acting of Nosawa Masako, Goku went through feelings of anger, sadness, not reconciled, all of these emotions combined into a loud scream, making him the one and only legendary Super Saiyan, what is known to be the strongest warrior in the universe. Many anime after this started to make characters go through transformations, or allow characters to have development under upset and anger feelings. Dragon Ball is the one that started this trend, this scene has always been part of the whole anime history. In addition, Toriyama used lighting and a darker sky to show the emotion of Goku, since Namek never has night time. The anger of Goku caused Namek to experience a weather that never happened before.
