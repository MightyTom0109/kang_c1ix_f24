Dragon Ball Z

Dragon ball z is where everything started, it is still my favorite anime, and the artworks from Akira toriyama still seem very impressive to me. The art style from this famous artist really encouraged me to attempt drawing when I was a kid. I started to mimic a lot of his drawings although it looked really messed up at first, but as time moved on I began to pay more attention to the lines, composition, the coloring, shading, these aspects one by one makes the beautiful character I love to this date. I practiced drawing straight lines without using a ruler, making shapes more precise, and attempted colors with various combinations because I don’t want to make a mimic of his work look bad. It was at some point I realized I am already into art as a major, because I loved the work created by this japanese manga artist.

Mimicing and Sketching

In the early days, I have been trying to mimic the drawings from the anime, or some images that you can find online about the anime. However it didn’t turn out very well. Since I am taking these drawings very seriously, it often takes a very long time to get the lines, shapes organized in the way I want them to be. I didn’t have the knowledge nor the skill to make this process more efficient, therefore, I ended up ruining a lot of paper because I used the eraser too much, it broke the paper. However, I was having fun, truly having fun. Everytime when a piece was complete, I felt I accomplished something, something amazing to myself, something I found myself perhaps actually good at. Obviously, I wasn’t thinking about making this my major at the time, but it was still something I could show off to my classmates and parents. I was only able to use pencils and colored pencils, since these materials were forgiving if mistakes were made, which I do a lot. In addition, we only had A4 sized printing paper at my house, materials such as markers, the ink would penetrate the page, making the drawing very uncontrollable for me at the time. From today’s perspective, I would think those drawings are pretty bad, some even unacceptable, but I am glad that’s how everything started.

After some time drawing anime characters using colored pencils, I have found some significant issues. As a kid I really wanted to shine in the class, and my drawings did help me a lot, but at some point I realized I am not making improvements. Despite having a lot of fun, I figured making the tenth drawing didn’t feel any different from the first one, some of which felt worse. It is still possible to get some drawing done in a quality I was happy with, but I wasn’t learning anything, and one drawing took too long to make, and most importantly, without reference images, I could only draw stick figures. With all of these aspects combined, I decided to take art classes outside of school. A decision that my parents were very surprised about. Like many other kids I didn’t really like school, I rather spent most of my time on the TV, so me actively wanting to learn something made them really happy.

My experiences with Sketching

Learning art usually starts with learning how to sketch and mimic, kind of what I am already doing, but more efficiently. Instead of drawing 2 dimensional characters, we focused more on real life objects. It started with drawing geometric objects, then to fruits, bottles, plates, human body parts, etc. These drawings allow us to get an understanding of space and lighting, how to apply shading to the drawing making the piece more realistic and three dimensional. To be honest, it was very hard for me at the beginning of the first few classes, since there weren’t any specific lines or shapes for me to follow. With real objects, I need to organize the composition myself while applying shading without using any colors. There were also many moments where I wanted to give up, since the reason I started to draw was because I love anime. Not only do we not touch anime related drawings in class but also everything was black and white. The works were very mentally draining, but my teachers were telling me if you want to succeed in art, this is only the fundamentals and is something you have to learn.

As mentioned earlier, making black and white sketches with pencils can be very mentally draining, and in the future if I were to major in art, only knowing how to sketch is not enough. After practicing and learning to sketch for three to four years, I began to proceed into different materials, including markers, oil painting, water coloring, crayons, etc. I am really glad that I made this decision, it fulfills my journey in art, while being very refreshing to the mind, brings tons of emotional value. In addition, despite me hating on making black and white sketches for all those years, I have to admit, practicing those sketches did help me a lot in learning other materials, because it all comes down to trying to get the right shape and form of object regardless of what kind of medium being used. In terms of drawing anime characters, the outlines process became significantly faster, and was able to make proper shading, and sometimes even using coloring materials.

My improvements

If someone were to ask, what do I enjoy the most, besides the feeling of accomplishment, it would be to see my improvements. Unlike other subjects, math for example, as you get better in the subject, the level of problem also proportionally increases, so it may seem quite unclear to judge self improvement, art on the other hand, improvements can be directly seen visually, especially when you compare the two work before and after. The feeling and exclamation of improving throughout the years, is a sign that you didn’t waste your time, and it feels great. To me, all these years of sitting in front of random objects, while experiencing different art materials allowed me to improve significantly not only in anime drawing, but as an art student in general I am more comfortable with communicating my thoughts visually.

Attitude changing and shift in focus

Since I started to apply for college, things have changed. Art now feels a lot different than before. It’s more than just a feeling of changing a hobby into a major, but it feels like I have been carrying more weight than before. Before, I drew for myself. I make drawings because I want to feel good about myself. It sounds very naive, but it was fun. It's ok to make mistakes, it's fine if I don’t complete the drawing. I only heard voices of encouragement and compliments. However, things started to feel different the moment when I started to make the portfolio for college. People were upset about my work, anime wasn’t accepted by many colleges in the US, and my mind was filled with deadlines. Professors only give feedback on the bad things, things you could do better, it's helpful, but depressing at the same time once I start to hear it too much. I now work purely on digital devices, learning how to make things with the software. It is what people do in work these days, but it lacks the creativity from drawing, and now when Imake work, I'm thinking about whether others like it, instead of would I Like it.

Questioning myself

I have questioned myself many times throughout the years. Taking art as a major, meaning that it would be quite limited as to what I can do in the future when I graduate. Before, I always thought I was the special kid in the class, super good at drawing, but things changed, as I grew up. More and more talented people show up around me, and soon I figured I am not the best artist in the grade anymore. There were moments where I felt discouraged, since I thought art was what made me special and standout from the grade. I was ok about not being the best at other subjects as long as I was the best at art, but when I realized I am not the best at any subject, I felt empty.




I questioned whether art was actually my thing, since my beginning was literally wanting to draw anime characters. I didn't have any tragic backstories, nor a great dream to accomplish. I worried about the future and the present, but forgot about all those times where I felt great because of drawing anime characters. Even to this date, I still have moments where I might not keep up with the rest of the class. Am I wasting time in this college, is this the proper major for me? All these concerns stress me out, but at the end of the day, one thing doesn’t change. Watching anime still makes me happy, no matter what context I am in.
